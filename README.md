basically would be learning how TCP segments flow, when a data is transferrred, the  data encapsulation takes place on the basis of OSI model , the data is encapsulated by the TCP HEADER the the IP HEADER then at last  the Ethernet HEADER(often called as the DLL (Data link layer i nthe OSI model)), after that, through NIC hardware (if connected via ethernet cables) data transfer in the form of signals, at every step there are MAC addresses changing, MAC address differs, a network in a same device is given a public IP suppose your home connection itself, thenit say somehow goes to the router and then from the router it goes to the ISP router at each tlevel the Ethernet Wrapper of the encapsulated data is fed with a new MAC address, it keeps on changing at every HOP, keeping the inside contents safe, 
So, why even we need a MAC address? 
The IP Address is your home mailing address. The postal service uses this to route the package across the country to your local post office.The MAC Address is your legal name and face. Once the delivery driver arrives at your apartment building, they look for your specific name on the mailbox to hand the package directly to you, rather than throwing it into a random apartment.
There are billions of IP addresses in the world. So, tracking and memorizing those would've been difficult memorising each of them, and thus we did this.
